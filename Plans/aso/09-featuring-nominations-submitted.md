## Track record

RetroRapid! has submitted multiple **Nominate Your App for Featuring** entries in App Store Connect. The approach has worked: the app has been **featured twice on the Updated and Upgraded list**.

| Nomination theme | Update led with | Featuring outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Accessibility Focused | v1.3 accessibility stack | **Featured** — Updated and Upgraded |
| Game Center Enhancements | Friend markers, challenges, achievements | **Featured** — Updated and Upgraded |

## Voice and style guide

RetroRapid featuring copy should sound like the submitted nominations below — confident, specific, and editor-friendly.

### Opening identity (one sentence)

Establish what RetroRapid is once, then pivot immediately to **this update**:

> *RetroRapid! is a retro LCD-style lane racer built from the ground up with accessibility at its core.*

Variants allowed when the story is not accessibility-led:

> *RetroRapid! is a retro LCD-style lane racer built from the ground up for every kind of player.*

Use **RetroRapid!** in new drafts unless reproducing archived ASC text verbatim.

### Lead with the update, not the catalog

- Name the version or campaign hook in the first paragraph (`Version 1.3`, `This update goes further`, `For GAAD, it introduces…`).
- Do not re-list every platform in the opening unless platform breadth is the story.
- Gameplay fantasy stays visible even in accessibility nominations — prove inclusion through **play**, not policy language.

### Prove claims with mechanics

Prefer concrete systems over adjectives:

| Weak | Strong (RetroRapid voice) |
|---|---|
| "Great accessibility" | Lane notes mapped to C, E, G; lane-change cues; multiple audio modes |
| "Works with VoiceOver" | Confident enough to declare VoiceOver support in Accessibility Nutrition Labels |
| "Social features" | Friend's Game Center avatar appears as a marker on the track at the moment you overtake them |
| "Lots of achievements" | Achievements tied to every control method, accumulated overtakes, and milestone scores |

### Sentence rhythm

- Mix short punches (`That's no small claim.`) with longer proof paragraphs.
- Use em dashes sparingly for emphasis, not every sentence.
- One rhetorical confidence beat works well: *Confident enough to declare… For a fast-paced arcade game, that's no small claim.*

### Community and credibility

When true and relevant, name external validation in **Helpful Details**, not Description — it saves Description bytes for gameplay proof:

- AppleVis blind and low-vision gaming community feedback
- Create with Swift newsletter — Indie App of the Week
- GAAD / accessibility occasion framing

Lead Helpful Details with `Community validation:` when used, then a blank line, then `Editor quick test:`. Do not invent press or community mentions.

### Mission closer (optional, consistent)

When space allows, close Description with the AMMEC line:

> *A share of proceeds supports AMMEC, advancing autonomy and social inclusion for people with physical disabilities.*

Keep it one sentence; do not let it replace gameplay proof.

### What to avoid

- Keyword stuffing or App Store metadata phrases (`arcade racer`, `high score`) — editors are not the search algorithm.
- Accessibility-only framing with no playable hook on slide one / paragraph one.
- Generic Game Center boilerplate without the **friend marker on track** moment.
- Repeating the full feature list from the App Store description.
- `RetroRacing` in user-facing nomination copy.

### Nomination type hints

| Story | Suggested ASC type |
|---|---|
| Accessibility stack, Dynamic Type, controllers, audio system | `App Enhancements` |
| Friend markers, challenges, achievements expansion | `App Enhancements` |
| GAAD badge, timed challenge, In-App Event aligned | `New Content` (only if event is approved) or `App Enhancements` |

## Submitted nominations

Archived copy as submitted to App Store Connect. Character counts recorded for future edits.

### 1. RetroRapid! — First update, Accessibility Focused

**Nomination name:** `RetroRapid! - First update, Accessibility Focused`

**Nomination type:** App Enhancements (inferred)

**Featuring outcome:** **Updated and Upgraded**

**Character counts (archived):** Description 998/1,000 ✅; Helpful Details 496/500 ✅

#### Description

```text
RetroRapid! is a retro LCD-style lane racer built from the ground up with accessibility at its core. Version 1.3 is its boldest update yet. Confident enough to declare VoiceOver support. For a fast-paced arcade game, that's no small claim. A custom audio system maps each lane to a distinct musical note (C, E, G), with lane-change cues and multiple audio modes letting blind and low-vision players build a mental map of the track and play independently. Direct Touch keeps reactions fast with VoiceOver on.

The update also adds Dynamic Type support, game controller compatibility, and runs natively across watchOS, iOS, iPadOS, and macOS. Control options include the Digital Crown, swipes, tap zones, and keyboard. Settings for haptics, volume, and fonts. Supports dark mode and both orientations. Reflecting a simple belief: accessible gaming means meeting players where they are. A share of proceeds supports AMMEC, advancing autonomy and social inclusion for people with physical disabilities.
```

#### Helpful details

```text
Community validation: the approach has been shaped by feedback from the AppleVis blind and low-vision gaming community, and it was recently featured as Indie App of the Week in the Create with Swift newsletter.

Editor quick test: enable VoiceOver, start a run, and use Direct Touch with tap zones or swipes to change lanes. Listen for lane notes (C/E/G) and lane-change cues; try audio modes and haptics in Settings. Also worth checking: Dynamic Type at the largest sizes, and both orientations.
```

**Why this worked:** Opens with product + version stakes; proves VoiceOver with audio architecture, not labels alone. Community validation sits in Helpful Details (saves Description bytes); editor test is a single playable path.

### 2. RetroRapid! — Game Center Enhancements

**Nomination name:** `RetroRapid! - Game Center Enhancements`

**Nomination type:** App Enhancements (inferred)

**Featuring outcome:** **Updated and Upgraded**

**Character counts (archived):** Description 971/1,000 ✅; Helpful Details 432/500 ✅

#### Description

```text
RetroRapid is a retro LCD-style lane racer built from the ground up for every kind of player. Game Center has always been part of it, with leaderboards by platform and speed level so competition stays fair. This update goes further.

As you race, a marker appears above the car that represents your friend's best score, showing their Game Center avatar. You see who you are chasing, at the exact moment you are overtaking them. The game over screen then shows which friend sits just ahead of you in the leaderboard, so the next run always has a specific target.

Challenges extend the competition further: achievements tied to every control method the game supports (Digital Crown, swipes, tap zones, keyboard), plus accumulated overtakes and milestone scores per run. There is always something left to chase, regardless of how long you have been playing.

A share of proceeds supports AMMEC, advancing autonomy and social inclusion for people with physical disabilities.
```

#### Helpful details

```text
Editor quick test: 1) Start a run and race until a friend's Game Center avatar appears as a marker on the track. That is the moment. 2) Finish the run and check the game over screen for the friend sitting just ahead of you in the leaderboard. 3) Open Game Center and explore leaderboards by platform and speed level. 4) Check Challenges and note achievements tied to each control method, accumulated overtakes, and milestone scores.
```

**Why this worked:** Centers the emotional hook — friend's avatar on track at the overtaking moment — before listing achievement taxonomy.

The interface adapts across watchOS, iOS, iPadOS, and macOS natively. And Dynamic Type, Dark Mode, portrait and landscape, game controller support…

#### Helpful details

```text
Editor quick test: 1) Enable VoiceOver and start a run, listen for lane notes (C, E, G) and notice how Direct Touch keeps reactions fast even with VoiceOver on. 2) Trigger the GAAD Challenge by completing a run with VoiceOver active during GAAD week. 3) Open Settings and explore audio modes, speed warning feedback, and haptics, the game communicates differently depending on your choices. 4) Crank Dynamic Type to its largest size and try both orientations. The game holds.
```

**Why this pattern works:** Ties a **timed challenge** to provable accessibility infrastructure; editor test step 2 validates the campaign hook directly.

## Template for future nominations

Copy this skeleton into App Store Connect and replace bracketed sections.

### Nomination name (≤ 60 characters)

```text
RetroRapid! — [Short update or occasion name]
```

### Description (≤ 1,000 characters)

```text
RetroRapid! is a retro LCD-style lane racer built from the ground up [with accessibility at its core / for every kind of player]. [Version X.Y / This update / For OCCASION] [one-sentence hook: what changed and why it matters].

[2–3 sentences of proof: specific mechanics, not feature labels. Name the player moment — what they see, hear, or feel.]

[Optional: platform breadth, controls, or secondary features — only if they support the hook.]

[Optional one sentence: community validation or press, if accurate.]

[Optional AMMEC closer.]
```

### Helpful details (≤ 500 characters)

```text
[Optional: Community validation: one sentence on AppleVis, press, or community shaping — only if accurate.]

Editor quick test: 1) [Primary wow moment — how to trigger it in under 60 seconds.] 2) [Secondary proof.] 3) [Settings or Game Center depth.] 4) [Accessibility or adaptability check, if relevant.]
```

For accessibility nominations with press/community proof, use the **Accessibility Focused** split: Description ends at AMMEC; Helpful Details opens with `Community validation:` then the editor quick test (see [§1 archive](#1-retrorapid--first-update-accessibility-focused)).

### Pre-submit checklist

- [ ] Description ≤ 1,000 characters; Helpful Details ≤ 500 characters
- [ ] Nomination Name ≤ 60 characters
- [ ] First paragraph names the **update**, not a generic app pitch
- [ ] At least one **specific mechanic** editors can verify in-app
- [ ] Helpful Details uses numbered **Editor quick test** steps
- [ ] Accessibility claims match shipped behavior and Nutrition Labels
- [ ] `RetroRapid!` spelling; no `RetroRacing` in public copy
- [ ] Supplemental URLs public and stable (see [06-gaad-featuring.md §9.8](06-gaad-featuring.md))
- [ ] Submit ≥ 3 weeks before publish window (≥ 4–5 weeks recommended)

## Editor quick test pattern

RetroRapid nominations that performed well share this Helpful Details shape:

0. **Optional lead — community validation** — when Description is near 1,000 characters, move press/community proof here (`Community validation: …`). Used in the featured Accessibility Focused nomination.
1. **Step 1 = the moment** — friend avatar on track, lane notes with VoiceOver, GAAD badge unlock. Tell the editor exactly when to look.
2. **Step 2 = the payoff** — game over friend target, audio modes, challenge completion.
3. **Step 3 = depth** — Game Center leaderboards by platform/speed, or Settings exploration.
4. **Step 4 = adaptability** — Dynamic Type largest size, both orientations, or control-method achievements.

Keep steps imperative (`Enable`, `Start`, `Listen`, `Open`). Avoid abstract instructions (`experience the accessibility`).
